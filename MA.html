MASTER OF ARTS

Master of Arts is a 2 years postgraduate degree course pursued by students after completion of a Bachelor’s degree in any relevant specialization. The top MA courses opted by students include MA in English, MA in History, MA in Political Science, MA in Economics, etc. Entrance exams are conducted for students to enroll in various MA courses which include JNUEE, IPU CET, DUET, PU CET, etc. The students get enrolled on the basis of merit as well in some colleges. Master of arts degree helps students to enhance their subject-specific knowledge. It provides an in-depth knowledge of the discipline they have selected. They also get an opportunity to attend Guest lectures and seminars by professionals in the academic circle or the business environment. Postgraduate courses have a larger curriculum and are more autonomous than UG courses. Students are required to put more effort into learning the entire process by developing new skills. The Masters’ degree instills the powers of inquiry, logical reasoning and critical analysis. Students can get a good position with their UG degree but for further advancement in the industry field, a Master's degree is a real turning point. Candidates who have a Bachelor's degree in art course or any special course with 50% marks(45% for reserved category), should pursue M.A degree. Candidates who want to pursue a Ph.D degree can opt for an M.A course. Students must be serious about their careers. The specialisation chosen will help to choose the career as students want. M.A syllabus is an academically oriented syllabus that includes theory and practical lessons, internships, research projects, and assignments. The syllabus and the course curriculum are different for every specialization. The core subjects that are taught in the first year of the curriculum.
